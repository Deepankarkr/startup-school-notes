# **Lecture Notes – How to Design Hardware Products (Hosain Rahman)**



---

## **1. Company Philosophy & Vision**

* Focus: **intersection of engineering + beauty**

* Goal: **better life through technology**

* Work in **Internet of Things (IoT)**:

  * Devices with **sensors + computing + connectivity**
  * Devices **talk to each other and users**

* Problem today:

  * Everything has apps → **fragmented experience**
  * Devices don’t communicate → **confusing for users**

* Core belief:

  * Shift from **devices → user-centered thinking**
  * Wearables = **“context engine”**

    * Always on body
    * Track: heart rate, respiration, behavior
    * Can inform other systems (car, thermostat, etc.)

---

## **2. Core Capability: Full Stack**

To realize vision, must be strong in:

* **Hardware**
* **Software**
* **Data**

Key points:

* Hardware must be **wearable 24/7**
* Software must be **engaging (like Instagram/WhatsApp)**
* Data must be **processed into useful insights**

→ Company = **hardware + software + data together**

---

## **3. Organizational Challenge**

* Hardware:

  * Slow cycles, physical constraints
* Software:

  * Fast iteration, flexible

Solution:

* Hardware learns **speed**
* Software learns **planning**
* Data science supports **decision-making**

---

## **4. System Thinking**

* Product = **complete system**, not parts

  * Hardware + App + Cloud + Platform + Developers

Example (UP product):

* Sensors → phone → cloud → insights → third-party apps

---

## **5. Product Creation Process**

### **Stages**

1. **Exploration**
2. **Validation**
3. **Concepting**
4. **Planning**
5. **Development**
6. **Launch + Learn + Iterate**

---

## **6. Exploration Phase**

* Very **creative, open-ended**
* Focus:

  * Vision, strategy, brand
  * Future possibilities

Activities:

* Demo Fridays
* Hackathons
* R&D-led

Decision rule:

* “Would I invest **$50K** in this idea?”

---

## **7. Validation Phase**

* Prove idea like **PhD thesis**
* Define **“WHY”**

  * Why it exists
  * What problem it solves

Focus:

* Feasibility (tech, cost, timeline)
* Business viability

---

## **8. Concept Phase**

* Owned by **Product Experience Team**
* Includes:

  * Industrial design
  * Software design
  * Audio design
  * Storytelling

Focus:

* Define:

  * **Hero experiences**
  * Product story
  * Differentiation

---

## **9. Planning Phase**

* Led by **Product Managers**
* Define:

  * Launch timeline
  * Trade-offs (battery, cost, features)

Key:

* Move from **ideal → realistic**

---

## **10. Development Phase**

* Engineering signs off:

  * “Can we build this?”

Focus:

* Solve constraints
* Fine details (important)

Examples:

* Jambox sound design took **months**
* Material quality (rubber, colors)
* UI animations carefully designed

---

## **11. Iteration**

* After launch:

  * Learn from users
  * Update product
  * Restart cycle

---

## **12. “WHY” Framework (Core Tool)**

Key question:

* **What problem do we solve that users can’t live without?**

Types:

* Known need OR
* Unknown need → becomes essential later

Example:

* Jambox:

  * Wireless speaker market = **0% → 78%**
  * Users didn’t ask for it → still succeeded

---

## **13. User Research Approach**

Avoid:

* “Do you want this feature?”

Instead ask:

* Behavior-based questions:

  * How do you listen to music?
  * When do you share?
  * How often?

Insight:

* Users **don’t tell you what to build**
* They help **refine your thinking**

---

## **14. Experience Framework (Jambox Example)**

### Problem:

* Media is on phone → need **portable, seamless audio**

### Strategy:

* Speaker = **entry into home ecosystem**

### Experience Continuum:

* Today → Tomorrow → Future
* Build products as **stepping stones**

---

## **15. System Design Principle**

* Company = **experience company**
* Solve problems using:

  * Hardware
  * Software
  * Cloud
  * UI
  * Sound

---

## **16. UP Product Framework**

### Core Idea:

* People know world → **don’t know themselves**

### Goal:

* Use sensors → **self-awareness + better decisions**

---

### **Track – Understand – Act Model**

1. **Track**

   * Collect data (hardware design critical)
2. **Understand**

   * Convert data → insights (context matters)
3. **Act**

   * Guide behavior (notifications, recommendations)

---

## **17. System Components**

* Hardware → data capture
* Cloud → processing
* App → experience
* Notifications → behavior change

---

## **18. Users & Personalization**

Different users:

* Weight loss
* Social validation
* Medical needs

Design:

* Different experiences per user type

---

## **19. Constraints Principle**

* Constraints help:

  * Simplify
  * Refine
  * Focus on real solution

---

## **20. Cross-functional Pods**

Each feature owned by:

* Product experience
* Hardware engineer
* Software engineer
* Data person

---

## **21. Decision Making (Trade-offs)**

* Must evaluate across **entire system**
* Not in silos

Method:

* Bring all teams together
* Discuss constraints openly
* Align with **core problem/goal**

---

## **22. Scaling Challenges**

* Company size: ~500 people
* Distributed teams (multiple cities)

Main issue:

* **Communication**

Solution:

* Frequent sync (daily long calls)
* Force cross-team alignment

---

## **23. Startup Advice**

* Think in **systems mindset**
* Even simple products are systems
* Focus on **interactions between parts**

---

## **24. Product Strategy**

* Build **grand vision**
* Create **stepping-stone products**

Consider:

* Market dynamics
* Replacement cycles
* Use cases

---

## **25. Iteration & Learning**

* Learn from failures (example: first UP failed)
* Feed insights back into system
* Run parallel innovation threads

---

## **26. Hardware vs Software**

* No existing model for combining both

Approach:

* Take best from both:

  * Software → speed, iteration
  * Hardware → precision, quality

---

## **27. Key Takeaways**

* Build **systems, not products**
* Focus on **user problem (“WHY”)**
* Combine **hardware + software + data**
* Use **constraints** to simplify
* Design for **emotional connection**
* Iterate continuously
