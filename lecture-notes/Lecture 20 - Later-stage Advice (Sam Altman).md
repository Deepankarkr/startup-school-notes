# **Lecture Notes – Later-Stage Advice (Sam Altman)**



---

# **1. Context**

* Focus: **post product-market fit**

* Relevant:

  * ~**25+ employees**
  * ~**12–24 months**

* Early stage:

  * Ignore most of this
  * Focus on:

    * **Product**
    * **Users**

---

# **2. Founder Role Shift**

### **Before PMF**

* Build **great product**

### **After PMF**

* Build **great company**

→ Biggest transition

---

# **3. Management**

### **Early stage**

* Flat structure
* Everyone reports to founder

---

### **Scaling stage**

* Need:

  * **Clear reporting lines**
  * One manager per employee

---

### **Avoid**

* No structure
* Overly complex structures

---

### **Rule**

* Keep:

  * **Simple**
  * **Clear**

---

# **4. Common Management Failures**

### **1. Not hiring senior people**

* Early: juniors OK
* Later: need **experienced leaders**

---

### **2. Hero Mode**

* Doing everything yourself
* Leads to **burnout**

Solution:

* Hire
* Accept short-term slowdown

---

### **3. Bad Delegation**

Wrong:

* Gather data → founder decides everything

Right:

* Give context → **employee decides**

---

### **4. Poor Personal Organization**

* Must track:

  * Tasks
  * People
  * Follow-ups

---

# **5. Documenting Company**

### Write down:

* **How things work**
* **Why (values/culture)**

---

### Benefit:

* Founder defines **company behavior**

---

# **6. HR (Important After Scaling)**

### Good HR = speed, not bureaucracy

---

### Key elements:

* Clear structure
* Career paths
* **Frequent feedback**
* Compensation tied to performance

---

### Compensation**

* Use **bands**
* Keep fair

---

# **7. Equity Strategy**

### Principle:

* Give **significant equity continuously**

---

### Recommendation:

* ~**3–5% per year** distributed

---

### Important:

* Use:

  * **Refresher grants**
  * Avoid vesting gaps

---

### Tools:

* Use proper **equity management system**
* Avoid Excel errors

---

# **8. HR Scaling Rules**

* At ~50 employees:

  * New compliance rules (training, etc.)

---

### Monitor:

* **Burnout**
* Shift from sprint → **marathon**

---

### Hiring**

* Add **recruiter**
* Define hiring process

---

### Onboarding**

* Structured first week
* Assign buddy

---

# **9. Diversity**

### Important early

* Avoid:

  * Homogeneous teams

---

### Clarification:

* Want:

  * **Diverse backgrounds**
* Not:

  * Different vision

---

# **10. Early Employees Problem**

* Early hires may not fit later roles

Solution:

* Discuss **career paths early**

---

# **11. Company Productivity**

### Problem:

* Communication overhead grows with team size

---

### Key concept:

* **Alignment**

---

### Ensure:

* Everyone knows:

  * Goals
  * Priorities

---

### Reality:

* Employees often don’t know goals

---

### Solution:

* Repeat goals constantly

---

# **12. Systems for Productivity**

* Roadmap (3–12 months)
* Clear goals
* Weekly management meetings
* Monthly all-hands
* Quarterly planning

---

### Extra:

* **Offsites** (strategy discussions)

---

### Rule:

* Focus on **product**, not process

---

# **13. Long-Term Company Building**

* Hardest problem:

  * **Repeatable innovation**

---

# **14. Operations (Later Stage)**

### Accounting**

* Move from chaos → structured
* Start audits

---

### Legal**

* Collect all documents
* Fix issues early

---

# **15. Founder Liquidity (FF Stock)**

* Set up later (Series B stage)
* Avoid early signaling issues

---

# **16. IP & Trademarks**

### Timing:

* Within **12 months of public disclosure**

---

### Action:

* File **provisional patents**
* Register trademarks

---

# **17. Financial Planning (FP&A)**

* Build detailed financial model
* Understand:

  * Business drivers

---

# **18. Fundraising Optimization**

* Consider:

  * Hiring **full-time fundraiser**

---

# **19. Tax Structure**

* Optimize early
* Can significantly reduce taxes

---

# **20. Founder Psychology**

### Reality:

* Gets **harder**, not easier

---

### Effects:

* Bigger highs
* Worse lows

---

### Also:

* Increased criticism

---

# **21. Long-Term Thinking**

### Advice:

* Commit for **10+ years**

---

### Benefit:

* Huge advantage over short-term thinkers

---

# **22. Burnout Prevention**

* Take vacations
* Avoid overwork

---

# **23. Losing Focus**

### Causes:

* Burnout
* Ego (events, fame)

---

### Rule:

* Stay **focused on company**

---

# **24. Acquisition Trap**

### Danger:

* Distraction
* Demotivation

---

### Rule:

* Only engage if willing to sell

---

# **25. Failure Reality**

### Truth:

* Startups die when founders **give up**

---

### Advice:

* Manage psychology
* Don’t quit prematurely

---

# **26. Marketing & PR**

### Early:

* Ignore

---

### Later:

* Important

---

### Founder role:

* Define **core messaging**
* Build **journalist relationships**

---

### Tip:

* Avoid PR firms
* Direct founder communication works better

---

# **27. Business Development**

### Now becomes important

---

### Principles:

1. Great product first
2. Build real relationships
3. Create competition
4. Be persistent
5. **Ask directly**

---

# **28. Startup Journey Reality**

### Pattern:

* Initial spike → drop → long struggle → growth

---

### Key:

* Long **“trough of sorrow”**

---

# **29. Failure Handling**

### If failing:

* Inform investors early
* Avoid sudden shutdown
* Support employees:

  * Jobs
  * Severance

---

# **30. Misc Advice**

### Hiring CEO

* Founders should remain CEO (usually)

---

### Raising money

* Wait until:

  * Idea works
  * Some traction exists

---

# **31. Core Takeaways**

* Shift: **product → company**
* Build:

  * Structure
  * Alignment
* Focus on:

  * People
  * Culture
* Think **long-term**
* Avoid:

  * Burnout
  * Distraction
* Success = **not giving up**
