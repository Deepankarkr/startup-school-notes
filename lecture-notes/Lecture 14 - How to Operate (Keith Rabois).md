# **Lecture 14: How to Operate (Keith Rabois)** 

---

## **Core Idea**

Operating = **building a high-performance machine (company)**

---

## **1. Company Building**

* Early stage = messy, fragile
* Goal → **self-sustaining system**

---

## **2. CEO Role**

* **Maximize output** of organization
* Focus on **results, not activity**

---

## **3. Reality of Work**

* Not glamorous
* Often:

  * Fixing small problems
  * Removing friction

---

## **4. Early Stage**

* Constant **triage**
* Identify:

  * Small problems (ignore)
  * Critical problems (fix fast)

---

## **5. Editor Framework**

### Your role = **Editor**

Key actions:

* **Simplify** (remove complexity)
* Ask **clarifying questions**
* Allocate **resources**
* Ensure **consistent voice**

---

## **6. Simplification**

* Reduce to **1–3 priorities**
* Complexity kills execution

---

## **7. Decision Making**

* Focus on **few critical variables**
* Ignore noise

---

## **8. Resource Allocation**

* Move people/resources to **highest impact areas**
* Both:

  * Top-down direction
  * Bottom-up ideas

---

## **9. Delegation**

### Key challenge:

* Delegate ≠ abdicate

Use:

* **Task-relevant maturity**

  * High skill → autonomy
  * Low skill → guidance

---

## **10. Decision Framework (Delegation)**

* High impact + high conviction → **you decide**
* Low impact + low conviction → **delegate**

---

## **11. Barrels vs Ammunition**

* **Barrels**:

  * End-to-end ownership
  * High leverage

* **Ammunition**:

  * Support execution

Rule:
→ Scale = more **barrels**, not just more people

---

## **12. Identifying Talent**

* Increase responsibility until it breaks
* Observe:

  * Who others go to for help

---

## **13. Hiring Strategy**

* Maintain **barrel : ammunition ratio**
* Too many people → no productivity gain

---

## **14. Focus Principle**

* Assign **one key problem per person**
* Avoid B-level work
* Push for **A-level problems**

---

## **15. Metrics & Dashboards**

* Define clear **success metrics**
* Everyone should understand them

---

## **16. Pair Metrics**

* Avoid optimizing one metric at cost of another

Example:

* Fraud ↓ vs false positives ↑

---

## **17. Look for Anomalies**

* Unexpected behavior → opportunity

Examples:

* PayPal → eBay users
* LinkedIn → profile views

---

## **18. Transparency**

* Share:

  * Metrics
  * Decisions
  * Meeting notes

Goal:
→ Alignment + trust

---

## **19. Consistent Voice**

* Company should feel like **one person wrote everything**
* Applies to:

  * Product
  * Website
  * Communication

---

## **20. Details Matter**

* Excellence in small things → big outcomes

Examples:

* Office environment
* Tools (laptops)
* Food quality

---

## **21. Culture Through Details**

* Early behavior → long-term culture
* High standards propagate

---

## **22. Effort**

* Requires **extreme effort**
* Founders must lead by example

---

## **23. Management Basics**

* Weekly/bi-weekly **1:1 meetings**
* Employee sets agenda
* Continuous feedback

---

## **24. Key Insight**

* Great companies =
  **focus + execution + systems + people quality**

---

## **Final Takeaways**

* Build **machine, not chaos**
* Simplify everything
* Hire and develop **barrels**
* Focus on **high-impact work**
* Details + culture = **performance**
