# **Lecture 4: Building Product, Talking to Users, and Growing (Adora Cheung)** 

---

## **Core Idea**

Goal: go from **0 → many users**. Most learning comes from **mistakes and iteration**.

---

## **1. Time & Focus**

* Need **long, uninterrupted time blocks**
* Avoid context switching (e.g., 2 hours daily < full-day focus)
* Deep immersion is critical

---

## **2. Wrong Approach (Common Mistake)**

* Hide idea → build in isolation → launch big
* Result: users come but **don’t stay** (no feedback loop)
* Leads to wasted time and failure

---

## **3. Start with Problem**

* Define problem in **one sentence**
* Ask:

  * Do I have this problem?
  * Do others have it?
* Validate by **talking to people early**

Mistake example: building for users you don’t relate to → no passion + poor product.

---

## **4. Understand the Industry**

* Immerse deeply (even short-term)
* Become a **participant** (not just observer)

Example:

* Worked as cleaners → learned inefficiencies
* Real insights come from **doing, not reading**

Also:

* Study competitors deeply (articles, financials, data)
* Look for **inefficiencies and opportunities**

---

## **5. Define Initial Users**

* Don’t target everyone
* Focus on **specific segment** (e.g., parents, students)
* Optimize product for them first

---

## **6. Design Before Building**

* Map full **user journey**:

  * Discovery → signup → usage → feedback
* Visualize **ideal experience** before coding

---

## **7. Build MVP**

* Smallest feature set solving core problem
* Avoid overbuilding

Also:

* Clear **one-line positioning**

  * Example: “Get your place clean for $20/hour”
* Users must understand instantly

---

## **8. Get First Users**

Start with:

* Yourself, co-founder
* Friends, family

Then:

* Communities (online/offline)
* Direct outreach (manual effort)

Example:

* Street fairs, direct selling → low conversion but necessary

---

## **9. Talk to Users**

* Provide contact channels (email, phone)
* Actively **reach out and talk**

Best method:

* Conversations (not surveys/interrogation)
* Build comfort → get honest feedback

---

## **10. Feedback Quality**

* Close people (family) → biased
* Random users → more honest (especially if paying)

Key insight:
→ **Paying users give best feedback**

---

## **11. Metrics**

Focus on:

* **Retention** (users coming back)
* Leading indicators:

  * Reviews
  * NPS

Retention curve:

* Initial drop → then flatten = good
* Continuous drop → bad

Goal: improve retention over time.

---

## **12. Build Strategy**

* Optimize for **next stage** (e.g., 10 → 100 users)
* Not for scale early

Principles:

* **Manual before automation**
* Learn processes first
* Avoid over-engineering

---

## **13. Avoid Mistakes**

* Don’t chase perfection
* **Temporary broken > no progress**
* Don’t blindly build all requested features
  → understand underlying problem first

---

## **14. Launch Early**

* Don’t stay in stealth
* Competitors will copy anyway
* Feedback > secrecy

---

## **15. Growth Strategy**

### Approach:

* Focus on **one channel at a time**
* Test → double down or move on
* Iterate continuously

---

## **16. Types of Growth**

### **1. Sticky Growth**

* Users keep coming back
* Driven by **good experience**
* Measured by:

  * Retention
  * CLV

---

### **2. Viral Growth**

* Users invite others

Needs:

* Great product
* Strong referral system:

  * Right timing (high engagement points)
  * Incentives (e.g., $10/$10)
  * Optimized signup flow

---

### **3. Paid Growth**

* Spend money to acquire users

Key formula:
→ **CLV > CAC**

* CLV = lifetime value
* CAC = acquisition cost

Also:

* Segment users (not all equal)
* Watch **payback time** (prefer < 12 months)
* Avoid unsustainable spending

---

## **17. Creativity in Growth**

* Growth is not just technical
* Requires **creative strategies**
* Find unique approaches others miss

---

## **18. Pivoting**

Pivot when:

* No growth
* Poor retention
* Bad unit economics

Guideline:

* If no growth for **3–4 weeks despite effort → reconsider**

Expected growth:

* Early: manual growth possible
* Later: need scalable strategies

---

## **19. Switching Users**

* Hard to replace existing habits

Strategy:

* Target moments where you are **clearly better**
* Focus on **1–2 strong advantages**, not many small ones

---

## **Final Takeaways**

* Start with **problem + users**, not product
* **Talk to users constantly**
* Build fast, iterate faster
* Growth = **retention + value + focus**
* Do things that **don’t scale early**
