# **Lecture 9: How to Raise Money (Marc Andreessen, Ron Conway, Parker Conrad)** 

---

## **Core Idea**

Fundraising = **byproduct of a strong company**, not the goal

---

## **1. What Investors Look For**

Key traits:

* **Strong founder** (leader, communicator)
* **Obsessed with problem** (prefer personal problem)
* Ability to **hire + execute**

Also:

* Clear **one-line explanation** of product

---

## **2. Venture Capital Reality**

* Extreme **outlier game**
* Few companies generate most returns

Insight:
→ Investors look for **exceptional strength**, not average quality

---

## **3. Invest in Strength**

* Don’t optimize for “no weaknesses”
* Big winners often have flaws

Rule:
→ One **extreme advantage** matters more

---

## **4. “Be So Good They Can’t Ignore You”**

* Best way to raise money → build strong company
* Pitch quality matters less than **traction/quality**

---

## **5. Fundraising is Not Success**

* It’s just a **step**, not achievement
* Harder work comes after

---

## **6. Bootstrap First**

* Avoid raising too early
* Strong businesses attract capital naturally

---

## **7. Risk & Fundraising (Onion Model)**

Startup = layers of risk:

* Team
* Product
* Market
* Growth
* Revenue

Funding purpose:
→ **Remove risks step by step**

Rounds:

* Seed → basic product + team
* Series A → product + early traction
* Later → scale

---

## **8. Fundraising Strategy**

* Raise money **against milestones**
* Match funding size to risk removed

---

## **9. Seed Stage Process**

* Intro via network
* Short summary → call → meeting
* Background checks → invest

Key:
→ Strong network matters

---

## **10. Series A Insights**

* Usually follows seed round
* Rare without prior traction

---

## **11. Choosing Investors**

Look for:

* Domain expertise
* Strong network
* Long-term support

Avoid:

* Investors only chasing returns

Insight:
→ Investor = **long-term partner (like marriage)**

---

## **12. Negotiation Basics**

* Don’t over-optimize valuation
* Focus on **right investors**

Typical:

* Seed: ~10–15%
* Series A: ~20–30%

---

## **13. Cap Table Discipline**

* Don’t give away too much early
* Over-dilution → founder demotivation

---

## **14. Practical Tips**

* No NDAs early
* Move fast
* Get commitments **in writing**
* Follow up clearly

---

## **15. Fundraising Process**

1. Build strong company
2. Get warm introductions
3. Pitch clearly
4. Close quickly

---

## **16. Common Mistakes**

* Over-focusing on pitch
* Ego in fundraising
* Raising too much money
* Poor investor selection

---

## **17. Control Dynamics**

* If company doing well → **founder control**
* If struggling → **investor control**

---

## **18. Board & Investors**

* Board rarely decides via voting
* Most decisions = alignment

Key:
→ Relationships matter more than structure

---

## **19. Key Insight**

Fundraising difficulty = signal

* Easy → strong business
* Hard → deeper problems ahead

---

## **Final Takeaways**

* Build **great company first**
* Raise money to **remove risk**
* Choose investors carefully
* Don’t over-focus on valuation
* Fundraising is **easy compared to execution**
