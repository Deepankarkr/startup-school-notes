# **Lecture 8: Getting Started, Doing Things that Don’t Scale, Press** 

---

## **Core Idea**

Startups begin with:
→ **experiments + speed + manual effort**

---

## **1. Start with Problem Discovery**

* Talk to users (e.g., small businesses)
* Identify **real pain points**

Example:

* Delivery problem discovered via interviews

---

## **2. Test Idea Quickly**

* Build simple prototype (landing page)
* Measure real behavior (calls/orders)

Insight:
→ If users tolerate bad UX → strong demand

---

## **3. Launch Fast**

* Don’t overbuild
* Launch in **hours, not months**

Goal:
→ Validate demand, not build system

---

## **4. Do Things That Don’t Scale**

Early stage:

* Be delivery driver
* Do customer support
* Manual operations

Examples:

* Google Docs for tracking
* Phone calls for orders

---

## **5. Benefits of Manual Work**

* Learn business deeply
* Understand users directly
* Discover real problems

---

## **6. Customer Interaction**

* Email every user
* Personalize communication

Effect:
→ Strong feedback + loyalty

---

## **7. Product-Market Fit Signal**

* Users come despite bad experience
* Organic growth (word of mouth)

---

## **8. Early Growth Principles**

* Ignore competition early
* Focus on **serving demand**

---

## **9. Key Lessons**

1. Treat startup as **experiment**
2. **Launch fast**
3. Do things that **don’t scale**

---

# **Part 2: First Users & Early Growth (Teespring)**

## **10. First Users**

* Hardest to acquire
* Require **manual effort**

Methods:

* Emails, calls, outreach
* No shortcuts

---

## **11. Avoid Free Users Trap**

* Free users ≠ real demand
* Paid users validate value

---

## **12. Turn Users into Champions**

* Deliver **exceptional experience**
* Go beyond expectations

---

## **13. Talk to Users Constantly**

* Customer support
* Reach out to churned users
* Monitor social channels

Rule:
→ Learn continuously from users

---

## **14. Fix Problems Fast**

* Mistakes inevitable
* Always **make it right**

Insight:
→ One bad user can destroy momentum

---

## **15. Find Product-Market Fit**

* Initial product ≠ final product
* Iterate fast

Rule:
→ Optimize for **speed over perfection**

---

## **16. Build for Next Stage**

* Focus on next milestone:

  * 10 → 100 → 1000 users
* Not long-term scaling early

---

## **17. Speed over Clean Code**

* Hack solutions
* Accept technical debt

Goal:
→ Reach PMF faster

---

## **18. Keep Doing Non-Scalable Work**

* Don’t stop too early
* It’s a **competitive advantage**

---

# **Part 3: Press & PR (Justin Kan)**

## **19. Press is Not Magic**

* Not merit-based
* Requires **strategy + effort**

---

## **20. Define Goal First**

Examples:

* Get users
* Reach specific audience
* Build investor perception

---

## **21. Types of Stories**

* Product launch
* Fundraising
* Milestones
* Stunts
* Hiring

---

## **22. Story Must Be Interesting**

* Ask: would others care?
* Needs novelty or timing

---

## **23. How to Get Press**

Process:

1. Define story
2. Get warm intro to journalist
3. Pitch clearly
4. Provide assets + follow up

Key:
→ Treat like **sales funnel**

---

## **24. Build Relationships**

* Maintain journalist contacts
* Help others → get help back

---

## **25. PR Firms**

* Expensive
* Limited value early

Better:
→ Do it yourself first

---

## **26. Limits of Press**

* Not scalable
* Short-term boost only

---

## **Final Takeaways**

* Start with **real problem + fast experiment**
* Do **manual, unscalable work early**
* Talk to users constantly
* Optimize for **speed + learning**
* Press = **tool, not growth engine**
