# **Lecture 7: How to Build Products Users Love (Kevin Hale)** 

---

## **Core Idea**

Build products users **love (not just use)** → growth = **conversion − churn**

---

## **1. Philosophy**

* Focus on **first user → first dollar**
* Get early experience right → everything scales

---

## **2. Product = Relationship**

Two phases:

* **New users → dating**
* **Existing users → marriage**

---

## **3. First Impressions (Dating)**

* First interaction determines retention
* Early mistakes = user leaves

Important “first moments”:

* Landing page
* Signup flow
* First login
* First email
* First support interaction

---

## **4. Emotional Design**

Two types of quality:

* **Functional (basic works)**
* **Delightful (emotional impact)**

Best products:
→ Combine both

Examples:

* Fun UI elements
* Personality in copy
* Delight in small details

Goal:
→ Make users **smile / feel something**

---

## **5. Memorable Experience**

* Users create **stories** about product
* Word-of-mouth driven by these stories

Example:

* Unique launches, creative campaigns

---

## **6. Long-Term Relationship (Marriage)**

Users leave because of:

* Money (pricing issues)
* Performance
* Trust
* Competition

→ Same problems as relationships

---

## **7. Biggest Mistake**

* Engineers disconnected from users
* Feedback loop broken

Fix:
→ **Everyone does customer support**

---

## **8. Support-Driven Development**

* Builders = support agents
* Immediate feedback → better product

Benefits:

* Faster bug fixes
* Better UX decisions
* Higher empathy

---

## **9. Customer Support Principles**

Avoid:

* Ignoring users (**worst mistake**)
* Defensiveness
* Contempt

Best practice:
→ Always respond, always engage

---

## **10. Learn from Users**

* Direct interaction required
* Reports/data not enough

Rule:
→ Regular exposure to users improves product quality

---

## **11. Reduce Knowledge Gap**

Two options:

1. Teach users more
2. Make product simpler (**better**)

Mistake:
→ Adding features increases complexity

---

## **12. Retention over Growth**

* 1% churn reduction = 1% conversion increase
* Easier to reduce churn

Wufoo growth:

* **No marketing → all word-of-mouth**

---

## **13. Show Users You Care**

* Constant improvements visible
* Communicate updates

Example:

* “Since you last logged in…”

Effect:
→ Users feel value continuously

---

## **14. Build Loyalty**

* Small gestures matter

Example:

* Handwritten thank-you notes

Impact:

* Strong emotional connection
* Better retention

---

## **15. Customer Intimacy Strategy**

Three paths to success:

1. Best price
2. Best product
3. **Best customer relationship**

Insight:
→ Third is easiest early-stage advantage

---

## **16. Product vs Marketing**

* Marketing = tax for weak product
* Best growth = **word of mouth**

Focus:
→ Build something people talk about

---

## **17. Handling Feature Requests**

* Don’t blindly build requested features
* Understand **underlying problem**

Approach:

* Build small → test → iterate

---

## **18. Culture & Execution**

* Everyone accountable
* Transparent work (simple to-do lists)
* Remote works with discipline

Key:
→ **Respect time + strong feedback loops**

---

## **Final Takeaways**

* Build for **emotion + usability**
* Talk to users constantly
* Fix feedback loop (support-driven dev)
* Reduce churn → drives growth
* Create product users **want to talk about**
