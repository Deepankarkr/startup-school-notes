# **Lecture 5: Competition is for Losers (Peter Thiel)** 

---

## **Core Idea**

Aim for **monopoly**, avoid competition.
→ **Competition destroys profits**

---

## **1. What Makes a Business Valuable**

Value =

* Create value (**X**)
* Capture value (**Y% of X**)

Key insight:

* X and Y are **independent**
* Big market ≠ big profit

Example:

* Airlines → large market, low profits
* Google → smaller market, high profits

---

## **2. Competition vs Monopoly**

Two types of businesses:

* **Perfect competition** → no profits
* **Monopoly** → high profits, stable

Competition:

* Efficient but no money
* High pressure, low margins

Monopoly:

* Durable advantage
* Can capture value

---

## **3. The “Lies” Companies Tell**

* Monopolies pretend they have competition
* Competitive companies pretend they are unique

Patterns:

* Weak companies → define market **too small**
* Strong companies → define market **too big**

---

## **4. Market Definition Matters**

Bad:

* Narrow fake niche (“British restaurant in Palo Alto”)

Good:

* Real, defensible category

Example:

* Google avoids saying “search monopoly” → says “tech/advertising company”

---

## **5. Start with Small Market**

Counterintuitive rule:
→ Start **small → dominate → expand**

Examples:

* Amazon → books
* Facebook → Harvard
* PayPal → eBay sellers

Avoid:

* Large markets early → too much competition

---

## **6. Monopoly Characteristics**

1. **Proprietary technology**

   * 10x better than alternatives

2. **Network effects**

   * Value increases with users

3. **Economies of scale**

   * High fixed, low marginal cost

4. **Brand**

   * Strong perception advantage

---

## **7. Last Mover Advantage**

* Not first → **last dominant player**

Examples:

* Google → last major search engine
* Microsoft → OS dominance
* Facebook → dominant social network

---

## **8. Value Comes from the Future**

* Most value = **future cash flows**
* Growth matters, but **durability matters more**

Key question:
→ Will this company still dominate in 10–20 years?

---

## **9. Technology vs Profit**

* Many innovations create value but **capture nothing**

Examples:

* Airlines, railroads → high value, low profit
* Scientists → create value, earn little

Lesson:
→ Structure of market determines profit, not innovation alone

---

## **10. Two Winning Models**

1. **Vertical integration**

   * Control entire system
   * Example: Tesla, SpaceX

2. **Software businesses**

   * Zero marginal cost
   * Fast scaling → strong monopolies

---

## **11. Speed Matters**

* Fast adoption → capture market before competitors
* Slow growth → invites competition

---

## **12. Competition is Misleading**

* People follow crowded paths (herd behavior)
* Competition = validation illusion

Reality:

* Crowded markets = bad opportunity

---

## **13. Psychological Trap**

* People compete by default
* Focus shifts from:

  * “What matters?” → “How to beat others?”

Result:

* Lose sight of real value

---

## **14. Key Strategy**

* Don’t enter crowded space
* Find **unique path** others ignore

Quote idea:
→ Don’t go through crowded door, go through empty gate

---

## **15. Key Takeaways**

* **Monopoly > competition**
* Start **small market**, dominate, expand
* Build **10x better product**
* Focus on **long-term durability**
* Avoid crowded markets → they signal **bad opportunities**
