## **Quick Q&A Insights**

* **Finding fast-growing markets**: trust your instincts; observe what you and peers use.

## **Context**

If **idea + product** are wrong, nothing else helps. This lecture covers **team (co-founders, hiring)** and **execution**.

---

## **Co-Founders**

Co-founder choice is **critical**. Biggest startup failure reason = **co-founder conflicts**. Never pick random people or “co-founder dating” → almost always fails. Best: people you know well (college, work).

Prefer:

* Known co-founder > solo founder > random co-founder
* 2–3 founders ideal

Traits:

* **Relentlessly resourceful**
* Tough, calm, decisive, creative (example: “James Bond type”)
* Technical co-founder needed for tech startups

Set **equity early**, near equal. Use **vesting (4 years, 1-year cliff)** to avoid issues if someone leaves. Resolve conflicts early. Avoid remote co-founders early → slows communication.

---

## **Hiring**

Early rule: **don’t hire unless necessary**. Small teams are better (less burn, complexity). Bad early hire can **kill the company**.

When hiring:

* Make it top priority (0% or ~25% time)
* Hire only **excellent people** (no compromise)
* Mediocre hires poison culture

Best sources:

* People you know / referrals (primary source)
* Go beyond comfort zone

Experience:

* Less important early → prefer **aptitude + belief**

Hiring criteria:

* Smart
* Gets things done
* Enjoy working with them

Best method: **work together on small project** before hiring.
In interviews: focus on past work, not brainteasers. Call references deeply.

Other traits:

* Good communication
* Risk tolerance
* Determination (“unstoppable”)

Framework: hire people you’d:

* Spend time with
* Be comfortable reporting to

---

## **Equity & Retention**

* Give ~**10% equity to first 10 employees**
* Be generous with employees, not investors

Retention:

* Keep employees **happy and valued**
* Avoid constant criticism
* Give credit to team, take blame
* Don’t micromanage

Motivation drivers: **autonomy, mastery, purpose**

---

## **Firing**

* Founders wait too long → mistake
* **Fire fast** if not working
* Clear pattern of poor performance (not small mistakes)

Also remove:

* Political behavior
* Persistent negativity

---

## **Execution**

Startups = **long grind of execution**, not ideas. Culture follows founders → you must execute well yourself.

CEO roles:

* Vision, fundraising, evangelizing, hiring
* **Set execution bar (critical)**

Execution =

1. Decide what to do
2. Get it done

---

## **Focus**

Most important skill. Many distractions → choose **2–3 priorities only**.

* Say **no** often
* Work on right things, not just hard

Use **clear goals** (visible, repeated). Poor communication → loss of focus.

Always focus on:

* **Growth + momentum**

Avoid distractions:

* PR, conferences, advisors

---

## **Intensity**

Startups require **high intensity**:

* No real work-life balance
* Must outwork competitors

Small improvements → huge outcomes (e.g., viral growth 0.99 vs 1.01).

---

## **Speed & Execution Style**

* Move fast + maintain quality
* Be **decisive** (indecision kills startups)
* Bias toward action

Best founders:

* Deliver results continuously
* Break big problems into small tasks

---

## **Operational Behavior**

* Respond quickly
* Show up in person when needed (“get on a plane”)
* Do whatever it takes

---

## **Momentum & Growth**

* **Top priority always**
* Losing momentum → company decline

Rules:

* Track metrics weekly
* Never ignore growth
* Avoid “we’ll grow later” thinking

If momentum drops:

* Don’t give speeches
* Get **small wins quickly** (e.g., sales)

Disagreements → ask users and follow them.

---

## **Competitors**

* Ignore competitors unless they have real product
* Don’t get distracted by PR

Focus on improving your own product.

---

## **Final Takeaways**

* **Co-founders and early hires decide success**
* Hire **slow, fire fast**
* Execution = **focus + intensity + speed**
* Maintain **growth and momentum always**
