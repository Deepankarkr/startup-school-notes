# **Lecture Notes – YC Startup Talk for Students (2022)**



---

# **1. YC Overview**

* YC helps startups with:

  * **Building product**
  * **Raising money**
  * **Hiring team**

---

### **Examples of YC companies**

* Dropbox
* Stripe
* Airbnb
* Instacart

---

# **2. Best Way to Learn Startups**

### **Advice**

* **Work at a startup**

---

### **Benefits**

* Work directly with founders
* Learn:

  * Engineering
  * Team management
  * Business operations

---

### **Reality**

* Startups give:

  * **More responsibility than you deserve**

---

# **3. Startup vs Big Tech (FAANG)**

### **Big companies**

* Specialists
* Slow decisions
* High scale, low visibility

---

### **Startups**

* Generalists
* Fast decisions
* High ownership
* Direct customer interaction

---

### **Risk**

* Big tech → **low risk**
* Startup → **low–medium risk** (depends on stage)

---

# **4. Startup Stages**

---

## **1. Seed Stage**

* 2–5 people
* Pre-product / pre-revenue
* Funding: ~$1–5M

---

## **2. Series A**

* Some traction
* Early product-market fit signals

---

## **3. Growth Stage**

* Clear PMF
* Scaling customers
* 50–500 employees

---

## **4. Scale Stage**

* Expansion (global, partnerships)
* 500+ employees
* Large funding (~$500M)

---

# **5. Choosing a Startup**

* Not all startups are same
* Choose based on:

  * Stage
  * Role fit
  * Risk tolerance

---

# **6. What to Focus on at a Startup Job**

### **Core rule**

* **Understand business + measure impact**

---

## **Marketing**

* Focus:

  * Funnel metrics
  * Conversion rates
* Avoid:

  * Vanity metrics (likes, posts)

---

## **Sales**

* Goal:

  * **Revenue**

---

## **Product**

* Think like:

  * **Mini CEO**
* Focus:

  * Growth
  * Retention
  * Revenue

---

## **Engineering**

* Focus:

  * **Business impact**
* Not just:

  * Completing tasks

---

## **Support**

* Metrics:

  * NPS
  * Response time
  * Process efficiency

---

# **7. Startup Principles (YC)**

### **1. Make something people want**

* Stay focused

---

### **2. Talk to users**

* Understand:

  * Problems
  * Needs

---

### **3. Do things that don’t scale**

* Example:

  * Direct outreach
  * Manual work

---

# **8. Example – Airbnb**

### **Early stage**

* Niche use case
* Simple product

---

### **Lesson**

* Don’t need full product
* Need:

  * **Proof + hustle**

---

# **9. What YC Looks For**

### **1. Domain expertise + passion**

* Must sustain **long-term effort**

---

### **2. Strong founders**

* Execution ability
* Learning speed

---

### **3. Technical capability (optional now)**

* Still useful, but not mandatory

---

### **4. Prototype**

* Not required
* But expected if technical

---

### **5. Not looking for**

* Immediate billion-dollar idea
* Looking for:

  * **builders who iterate**

---

* Learn startups by:

  * **working in one**
* Focus on:

  * **impact**
