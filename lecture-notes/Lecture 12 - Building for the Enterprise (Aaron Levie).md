# **Lecture 12: Building for the Enterprise (Aaron Levie)** 

---

## **Core Idea**

Enterprise = **huge market + big opportunity** (if done right)

---

## **1. Consumer vs Enterprise**

* Consumer:

  * Hard to monetize
  * Limited revenue models

* Enterprise:

  * **$3.7T market**
  * Focus on **productivity/value**, not cost

---

## **2. Why Enterprise Was Hard**

* Slow product cycles
* Long sales cycles
* Complex software
* Poor UX
* Heavy sales dependency

---

## **3. What Changed (Big Shift)**

### Key shifts:

* **Cloud computing** → cheaper, faster deployment
* **Standard platforms** (less customization)
* **Global access** (serve all company sizes)
* **Mobile + user-led adoption**

---

## **4. New Model**

Old:

* IT-driven buying

New:
→ **User-driven adoption → then enterprise sales**

---

## **5. Massive Opportunity**

* Every industry becoming **tech-driven**
* Enterprises need new software to adapt

Examples:

* Retail → omni-channel
* Healthcare → personalized care
* Media → on-demand distribution

---

## **6. Key Strategy #1: Spot Disruptions**

Look for:

* Tech becoming cheaper/faster
* Gap between **what’s possible vs current state**

---

## **7. Key Strategy #2: Start Small**

* Find **narrow use case (wedge)**
* Solve it extremely well
* Expand over time

Example:

* Payroll → full HR platform

---

## **8. Key Strategy #3: Exploit Asymmetry**

Do things incumbents:

* **Can’t do (technical limits)**
* **Won’t do (business model constraints)**

---

## **9. Key Strategy #4: Business Model Innovation**

Example:

* Zenefits → paid by insurers, not customers

Insight:
→ Different monetization = advantage

---

## **10. Key Strategy #5: Find Edge Customers**

* Work with **early adopters (future users)**
* Build for advanced use cases

---

## **11. Listen to Customers (But Carefully)**

* Understand problems
* Don’t blindly build requests

Rule:
→ Simplify complex needs

---

## **12. Build Platform, Not Custom Product**

* Avoid heavy customization
* Use APIs + modular design

---

## **13. Consumer-Grade UX**

* Simple, intuitive product
* Drives adoption

---

## **14. Product-Led Growth + Sales**

* Product should **drive adoption**
* Sales helps scale + close deals

---

## **15. Enterprise Sales Reality**

* Still required
* More consultative, less pushy

---

## **16. Key Insight**

Enterprise success =

* **Great product + strong distribution + right timing**

---

## **Final Takeaways**

* Enterprise = **massive opportunity**
* Start **small → expand**
* Focus on **user experience**
* Leverage **tech shifts (cloud, mobile)**
* Combine **product-led + sales-led growth**
