# **Lecture 11: Hiring and Culture, Part 2 (Stripe, Pinterest)** 

---

## **Core Idea**

Culture = **how decisions scale when founders can’t be involved**

---

## **1. What Defines Culture**

Key dimensions:

* Who you **hire**
* What you **do daily**
* What you **communicate**
* What you **celebrate**

---

## **2. Culture as Scaling Mechanism**

* Founders can’t control everything
* Culture = **decision framework at scale**

---

## **3. Transparency (Stripe)**

* Share information widely
* Align everyone on mission + context

Benefits:

* Better decisions
* Less need for meetings

Challenge:

* Hard to scale → need tools + norms

---

## **4. Early Hiring Principles**

### How to find people:

* Friends, networks, events, cold outreach
* Seek **undervalued talent**

---

### What to look for:

* **High integrity, low ego**
* Curious, creative
* Care deeply
* Finish things

---

## **5. First 10 Hires**

* Most important hires
* Each hire = multiplier effect

Insight:
→ First 10 → define next 100

---

## **6. Hiring Strategy**

* Think like **value investor (find hidden talent)**
* Don’t rely only on resumes
* Work with candidates before hiring

---

## **7. Identifying Talent**

* Learn what “great” looks like
* Ask experts
* Use structured evaluation

---

## **8. References**

* Focus on:

  * What they’re best at
  * Weaknesses
  * Ranking vs peers

Goal:
→ Understand **real working behavior**

---

## **9. Selling the Company**

* Strong **vision** attracts talent
* Be honest about difficulty

Rule:
→ Right people choose **challenge**, not certainty

---

## **10. Onboarding & Early Execution**

* Get people doing **real work immediately**
* Give continuous feedback

---

## **11. Scaling Teams**

* Create **small autonomous teams**
* Reduce coordination complexity

---

## **12. Growth Challenges**

* Complexity grows fast
* Must design systems + structure

---

## **13. Time Horizon Shift**

* Early → short-term focus
* Later → long-term planning

---

## **14. Hiring Evolution**

* Early: immediate contributors
* Later: long-term potential

---

## **15. Culture Maintenance**

* Build strong social bonds
* Use rituals (meals, interactions)

---

## **16. Leadership Development**

* Some early hires grow → leaders
* Others remain individual contributors

Rule:
→ Give opportunities, not forced paths

---

## **17. Key Hiring Traits (Summary)**

* Genuine
* Care deeply
* High standards
* Finish work

---

## **18. Mission Alignment**

* Hire people excited by **problem/mission**
* Not just company stage

---

## **19. User-Driven Hiring**

* Early users → great hires
* Passionate users = strong fit

---

## **20. Key Insight**

* Companies ≠ founders
* Success = **team execution (99% work)**

---

## **Final Takeaways**

* Culture enables **scaling decisions**
* First hires define company DNA
* Hire for **values + potential**
* Transparency + communication = alignment
* Build systems as company grows
