# **Lecture 16: How to Run a User Interview (Emmett Shear)** 

---

## **Core Idea**

User interviews = **learn problems, not features**

---

## **1. Why User Interviews Matter**

* Avoid building wrong product
* Replace guesses with **real insights**

---

## **2. Biggest Early Mistake**

* Not talking to users
* Building for **yourself only** (rarely scales)

---

## **3. Who to Talk To**

* Most important decision

Types:

* Current users
* Competitor users
* **Non-users (critical)**

---

## **4. Key Insight**

* Majority of market = **non-users**
* Growth comes from converting them

---

## **5. Example (Twitch)**

* Users followed **content creators**, not platform
  → Focus shifted to **broadcasters**

---

## **6. How Many Interviews**

* ~6–8 interviews → patterns emerge
* After that → diminishing returns

---

## **7. Interview Goal**

* Understand:

  * Behavior
  * Pain points
  * Context

NOT:

* Feature requests

---

## **8. What NOT to Do**

* Don’t ask:

  * “Is this feature good?”
* Don’t show product early
* Don’t lead answers

---

## **9. What TO Ask**

* “How do you do this today?”
* “Why?”
* “Tell me more”

---

## **10. Interview Technique**

* Be **curious, not persuasive**
* Let users **talk freely**
* Follow unexpected insights

---

## **11. Detect Real Problems**

* Look for:

  * Friction
  * Workarounds
  * Repeated pain

---

## **12. Feature Trap**

* Users suggest **incremental ideas**
* Not breakthrough solutions

---

## **13. Validate Ideas**

Options:

1. Build quick prototype
2. Hack simple version
3. Get users to **pay (strongest signal)**

---

## **14. Best Signal**

→ Users willing to **pay or switch**

---

## **15. Compare User Groups**

Example (Twitch):

* Current users → feature requests
* Competitor users → missing capabilities
* Non-users → barriers to adoption

---

## **16. Key Learning**

* Real insights come from:
  → **why they don’t use your product**

---

## **17. Common Mistakes**

* Talking to easy users
* Asking wrong questions
* Overvaluing feedback
* Not recording interviews

---

## **18. Recording Interviews**

* Helps:

  * Share insights
  * Convince team

---

## **19. Data vs Interviews**

* Data → what happens
* Interviews → **why it happens**

---

## **20. Continuous Process**

* Keep talking to users
* Users change over time

---

## **21. Key Insight**

* Most ideas are wrong
* Interviews help **fail fast before building**

---

## **Final Takeaways**

* Talk to **right users (especially non-users)**
* Focus on **problems, not features**
* Use **small experiments to validate**
* Combine **interviews + data**
* Keep learning continuously
