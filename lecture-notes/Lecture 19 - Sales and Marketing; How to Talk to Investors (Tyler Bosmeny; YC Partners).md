# **Lecture Notes – Sales & Marketing; How to Talk to Investors**



---

# **PART 1: SALES (Tyler Bosmeny)**

---

## **1. What Sales Really Is**

* Sales ≠ charm or tricks

* Reality:

  * Founder = **salesperson**

* Core idea:

  * “Talking to users” = **selling**

---

## **2. Founder Advantage in Sales**

* **Passion for product**
* **Deep problem understanding**

→ More valuable than sales experience

---

## **3. Sales Funnel**

Stages:

1. **Prospecting**
2. **Conversations**
3. **Closing**
4. **Revenue**

---

## **4. Prospecting**

### **Key Insight**

* Only **~2.5% (innovators)** will try early product

→ Sales = **numbers game**

---

### **Methods**

1. **Personal network**
2. **Conferences**

   * Go where users are
   * Pre-email attendees
3. **Cold email**

---

### **Cold Email Template**

* Short:

  * Who you are
  * What you build
  * Ask for call

---

## **5. Sales Calls**

### **Most Important Rule**

* **Talk less, listen more**

Best salespeople:

* Talk **~30%**
* Listen **~70%**

---

### **Ask Questions**

* Why did you take this call?
* How do you solve this today?
* What’s ideal solution?

---

## **6. Follow-up**

### **Reality**

* Deals require **many follow-ups**

Example:

* Multiple emails, calls, reminders

---

### **Rule**

* Be **persistent**
* Push to:

  * **Yes OR No**

---

### **Avoid**

* “Maybe” pipeline → waste of time

---

## **7. Closing**

### **Process**

* Contracts → **redlining**
* Use standard templates

---

### **Key Principle**

* Goal = **close deals fast**

Avoid:

* Endless legal debates

---

## **8. Common Sales Traps**

### **1. Feature Trap**

* “We’ll buy if you add feature”

Reality:

* Usually **not real demand**

Solution:

* Get commitment OR ignore

---

### **2. Free Trial Trap**

* Free trials → no commitment

Solution:

* Offer:

  * Paid plan + **opt-out period**

---

## **9. Scaling Sales**

### **Think Early**

* What is **repeatable?**

---

### **Business Types**

* High price → few customers
* Low price → many customers

---

### **Important**

* Sales model must match:

  * Pricing
  * Scale

---

## **10. Key Takeaways (Sales)**

* Founder must **sell**
* Sales = **listening**
* Do **high volume outreach**
* Follow up aggressively
* Avoid fake signals (features, free trials)
* Move from **unscalable → scalable**

---

# **PART 2: PITCHING TO INVESTORS (Michael Seibel + YC)**

---

## **11. Core Principle**

* Best pitch = **better company**

---

## **12. 30-Second Pitch**

### **Structure (3 sentences)**

1. What you do
2. Market size
3. Traction

---

### **Rules**

* Use **simple language**
* Anyone (even parents) should understand

---

## **13. 2-Minute Pitch**

Add:

### **1. Unique Insight**

* What others don’t know
* Must create **“aha” moment**

---

### **2. Business Model**

* One clear answer
* Avoid multiple options

---

### **3. Team**

Focus on:

* Number of founders (2–4 ideal)
* Technical vs business
* History together
* Full-time commitment

---

### **4. Ask**

Be clear:

* Amount raising
* Instrument (SAFE / note)
* Terms

---

## **14. When to Fundraise**

### **Rule**

* Raise when **strong**

---

### **Strength Signals**

* Traction
* Investor interest
* Fast progress

---

### **Avoid**

* “We need money to start”

---

### **Mindset**

* Build anyway → investors join

---

## **15. Setting Investor Meetings**

### **Rules**

1. **Warm introductions**
2. Run process in **parallel**
3. Schedule all meetings together

---

### **Trick**

* Delay meetings → batch them

---

### **Ownership**

* One founder handles fundraising

---

## **16. Pitch Mistakes (Bad Example)**

Common errors:

* Unclear product
* Vague numbers
* No insights
* Weak team story
* No direction

---

## **17. Good Pitch Characteristics**

* Clear narrative
* Real user examples
* Specific traction
* Honest gaps
* Strong ask

---

## **18. After Investor Meeting**

### **Follow-up**

* Always follow up

---

### **Reality**

* Anything ≠ money → **No**

---

### **Create “Deal Heat”**

* Multiple investors interested

---

### **Do Due Diligence**

* Evaluate investors too

---

### **Know When to Stop**

* Fundraising ≠ success

---

## **19. Key Takeaways (Investors)**

* Keep pitch **simple**
* Focus on **traction**
* Show **insight**
* Be clear on **ask**
* Fundraising is **secondary to building**

---

# **FINAL CORE MESSAGE**

* Sales = **talk to users**
* Pitch = **explain simply**
* Fundraising = **not the goal**
* Success = **building a strong company**
