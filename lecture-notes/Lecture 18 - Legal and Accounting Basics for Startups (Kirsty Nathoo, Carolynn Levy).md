# **Lecture Notes – Legal & Accounting Basics for Startups**



---

## **1. Purpose of Lecture**

* Covers **basic legal + accounting mechanics**
* Goal:

  * Know **basics**
  * Avoid **common mistakes**
  * Focus on **building company**

---

## **2. Startup Basics**

A startup involves:

* **Legal entity**
* **Assets (IP, code, inventions)**
* **Fundraising**
* **Employees**
* **Contracts**

Also important:

* Founder roles
* Equity split

---

## **3. Company Formation**

### **Why form a company?**

* Protect from **personal liability**

  * Lawsuits affect company, not personal assets

---

### **Where to incorporate?**

* Best choice: **Delaware**

  * Standard
  * Clear laws
  * Investor-friendly

**Lesson:**

* Don’t experiment → **keep it standard**

---

### **How to form?**

Steps:

1. File basic documents → creates **company shell**
2. Set up:

   * **Bylaws**
   * **Board of directors**
   * **Officers (CEO, Secretary)**
3. Assign:

   * IP, code → **company ownership**

---

### **Key concept**

* Always separate:

  * **You (individual)**
  * **Company (entity)**

---

### **Tools**

* Use services like **Clerky**
* Avoid complex setups

---

### **Documents**

* Must:

  * **Sign**
  * **Store safely**

Important during:

* Fundraising
* Acquisition

---

## **4. Equity Allocation**

### **Key idea**

* Equity = **company pie**

---

### **Principles**

* **Execution > idea**
* Ideas alone = **zero value**

---

### **Founder split**

* Prefer:

  * **Equal or near-equal split**

Red flag:

* Large imbalance → trust issues

---

### **Focus**

* Think **future contribution**, not past

---

### **Insight**

* Top companies:

  * **No major unequal splits**

---

## **5. Stock Ownership**

### **Reality**

* Talking ≠ owning

You must:

* Sign **stock purchase agreement**

---

### **Payment**

* Shares in exchange for:

  * Cash OR
  * IP contribution

---

### **Critical: 83(b) Election**

* Must file
* Cannot fix later
* Missing it → deals can fail

---

## **6. Vesting**

### **Definition**

* Ownership earned **over time**

---

### **Standard**

* **4 years vesting**
* **1 year cliff**

---

### **Example**

* Leave before 1 year → **0 shares**
* After 1 year → **25% vested**

---

### **Why vesting?**

* Prevent:

  * Founder leaving with large equity
* Ensure:

  * **Skin in the game**

---

### **Applies to**

* Even **solo founders**

---

## **7. Fundraising Basics**

### **Two types**

1. **Unpriced (Seed)**

   * No valuation
   * Uses:

     * **SAFE**
     * Convertible notes

2. **Priced (Series A, B)**

   * Valuation fixed

---

### **SAFE concept**

* Investor gives money now
* Gets shares later

---

### **Valuation Cap**

* Upper limit for future share price
* Early investors get:

  * **Better deal**

---

### **Example**

* $100K at $5M cap → more shares than later investors

---

## **8. Fundraising Risks**

### **Dilution**

* Example:

  * Early investors: 25%
  * New investors: 20%
    → Founders lose **45%**

---

### **Investor quality**

* Prefer:

  * **Sophisticated (accredited) investors**

Avoid:

* Small, uninformed investors → cause issues later

---

### **Rule**

* Some money at low valuation > no money

---

## **9. Investor Terms to Understand**

### **1. Board Seat**

* Usually avoid
* Only accept if **high value**

---

### **2. Advisors**

* Investors already help
* Don’t give **extra shares**

---

### **3. Pro-rata Rights**

* Investors maintain % ownership
* Leads to:

  * More **founder dilution**

---

### **4. Information Rights**

* Investors want updates
* Monthly = OK
* Overreach = NOT OK

---

### **Key rule**

* Understand **all terms**, not just valuation

---

## **10. Business Expenses**

### **Definition**

* Costs to run business:

  * Salaries
  * Rent
  * Marketing

---

### **Rules**

* Deductible → reduces taxes
* Non-business → not deductible

---

### **Separation**

* Company money ≠ personal money

---

### **Guideline**

* If embarrassed explaining expense → **not valid**

---

### **Important**

* Keep:

  * Receipts
  * Records

---

## **11. Founder Employment**

### **Reality**

* Founders = **employees**

---

### **Rules**

* Must:

  * Pay salary (at least minimum wage)
  * Pay payroll taxes

---

### **Risk**

* Not paying → legal + financial issues

---

### **Founder breakups**

* Unpaid founders create:

  * Legal leverage
  * Conflict

---

### **Lesson**

* Treat founder terms like:

  * **Prenup**

---

## **12. Hiring Employees**

### **Employee vs Contractor**

#### Contractor:

* Flexible hours
* Own tools
* Paid without tax withholding
* Gets **1099**

#### Employee:

* Fixed structure
* Taxes withheld
* Gets **W-2**

---

### **Requirements**

* Assign IP to company
* Verify work authorization
* Provide:

  * Workers’ comp insurance

---

### **Recommendation**

* Use **payroll service** (e.g., ZenPayroll)

---

## **13. Firing Employees**

### **Best Practices**

1. **Act fast**
2. Be **direct**
3. Pay dues immediately
4. Remove system access
5. Handle equity (repurchase unvested shares)

---

### **Key**

* Professional handling = **good founder skill**

---

## **14. Accounting**

### **Roles**

* **Bookkeeper** → tracks expenses
* **CPA** → files taxes

---

### **When needed**

* CPA → within first year

---

### **How to find**

* Use:

  * **Startup-focused referrals**

---

## **15. Key Metrics**

Founders must know:

* **Cash position**
* **Burn rate**
* **Runway**

---

## **16. Core Takeaways**

* **Keep it simple**
* Use **standard structures**
* Maintain **documents**
* Understand **equity + dilution**
* Always **sign paperwork**
* Pay:

  * Founders
  * Employees
* Assign **IP to company**
* Handle hiring/firing properly
* Know **financial basics**
